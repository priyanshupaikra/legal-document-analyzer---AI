Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) Analysis

Example of a typical business NDA

Sample Clause

"The Receiving Party agrees that the Proprietary Information will remain the sole and exclusive property of the Disclosing Party, and that the Receiving Party will not acquire any ownership rights therein..."

AI Analysis

Ownership Clause

Plain Language Summary: This clause states that any confidential information you receive remains the property of the company that shared it with you. You don't gain any ownership rights to it.

Low Risk

Key Insights

  • Clear Ownership Definition: Explicitly states that proprietary information remains with the disclosing party.
  • Standard Industry Practice: This is a common and expected clause in most NDAs.
  • Suggestion: Ensure you understand what constitutes "Proprietary Information" as defined in the full agreement.

Employment Contract Analysis

Example of a software developer employment contract

Sample Clause

"Employee agrees that during the term of employment and for a period of 2 years following termination, Employee will not directly or indirectly solicit any client or customer of the Company..."

AI Analysis

Non-Solicitation Clause

Plain Language Summary: You can't try to get the company's clients to work with you or your new company for 2 years after leaving.

Medium Risk

Key Insights

  • Extended Duration: 2-year restriction period is longer than the industry standard of 1 year.
  • Broad Scope: Covers both direct and indirect solicitation of clients.
  • Suggestion: Negotiate for a 1-year period or request clearer definitions of "client" and "customer".

Service Agreement Analysis

Example of a web development service agreement

Sample Clause

"The Company shall not be liable for any indirect, incidental, special, consequential or punitive damages, including but not limited to lost profits..."

AI Analysis

Limitation of Liability Clause

Plain Language Summary: The company won't pay for indirect damages like lost profits if something goes wrong with their service.

High Risk

Key Insights

  • Broad Exclusion: Excludes almost all types of damages beyond direct losses.
  • Standard Clause: Common in service agreements to protect the service provider.
  • Suggestion: Consider negotiating a cap on liability or requesting additional insurance coverage.
